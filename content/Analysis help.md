So rather than simply describing your 10-15 examples, you need to critically dig deeper by asking specific questions:

- What specific aspects of these designs are working, and how?
- What are the underlying mechanics and design components?
- Why and how do these examples best represent your chosen word/state?
- How do these designs create their particular effects?
- What research supports how these mechanics function within the designs?
- How might you apply similar mechanics in your own design work?
- What is your own 'analysis', and how do these examples connect to your personal practice and project ideas for this semester?
Essentially, as designers  want to be moving beyond surface-level description to genuine interrogation of the design choices and their impacts, and then looking at research underpinning how these designs impact us on a sensory, emotional and cognitive levels.
