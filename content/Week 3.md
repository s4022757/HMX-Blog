## Academic

### Bethalmy & Corraliza 2019 - Transcendence and Sublime Experience in Nature: Awe and Inspiring Energy

This research establishes an operational definition and a reliable measurement tool for the powerful emotional experience of the sublime.

The author's reveal that this transcendent feeling comprises two distinct, yet related, components: awe and inspiring energy. Awe involves feelings of fear, vulnerability, and respect toward nature's vastness, while inspiring energy encompasses positive emotions like vitality, joy, freedom, and a sense of oneness with the universe.

The research revealed an unexpected paradox: feelings of awe, despite their profound nature, are positively linked to indifference toward the environment. The central point is that intense emotional states, like awe or inspiring energy, are often personal experiences that enhance well-being but do not reliably predict (or cause) action toward broader causes like environmental protection. 

### Mathias - Mediating the Sublime 2025

Mathias explores the concept of the sublime as a powerful and productive theoretical framework for designing immersive mediation and exhibitions in museum settings. The author argues that the sublime, defined as a mixed aesthetic experience where terror and pleasure intertwine, offers a way to reconcile the intense sensory qualities of immersion with the critical demands of meaning-making and ethical reflection.

The paper provides analysis of the exhibition _Goya and Munch: Modern Prophecies_ at the MUNCH museum, where the characteristics of the sublime, overwhelming quantity, inability to be shown, and emotional weight, were deliberately employed in designing the flow between scenes and the major dramatic settings.
