Continuing on learning touch designer, let's try to find a better tutorial for creating an audio reactive project with a closer visual result to what I am aiming for. Anything Ikedaesque is surely on the money.

<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/RocOuZhGvtY?si=pMw-tGu5V4dmw-x-" title="YouTube video player" frameborder="0" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture; web-share" referrerpolicy="strict-origin-when-cross-origin" allowfullscreen></iframe>

Well, this demonstrates other directions for implementing audio reactive networks, into a simpler and effective result. The connections between the parameters and nodes is working harder and in more nuanced ways. Recreating this project has definitely proved that the possibilities with this program are certainly impressive.

![[Screenshot 2025-11-14 at 6.58.39 am.png]]

Unfortunately, the visual result is again not what I'm looking for. While the audio reactive component is certainly there, the visuals that it is driving are quite simple. While this could be a great way to create the background, I'm still unsure about how to use this to create the void form that I am trying to achieve.

Let's follow another tutorial exploring how complex geometric shapes can be created.

<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/Mt2hwb5cngA?si=x9R0ZyChS5_Xi6KM" title="YouTube video player" frameborder="0" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture; web-share" referrerpolicy="strict-origin-when-cross-origin" allowfullscreen></iframe>

Now this has unlocked a great potential. By creating a cylinder, turning it into a geometric node network, and then manipulating and warping it, I was able to create exactly the form I was searching for.
![[Screenshot 2025-11-14 at 7.07.47 am.png]]

By importing the sound design I made for the earlier draft, I can see the shape morph and respond along to my own sound design. On top of this, by varying the parameters of this Noise TOP, I am able to manually drive and amplify the audio reactive effect.

I can even keyframe the parameters along the timeline of the audio, and I went ahead and animated the void to approach the screen in the beginning, and really pushed the amplitude of the morphing in synch with the introduction of sonic elements as the sound builds.
![[Screenshot 2025-11-14 at 7.12.42 am.png]]
