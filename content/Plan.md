## ✍️ Draft Outline for Posts 1, 2, and 3

Here are drafts to get you started, focusing on the first phase of the semester.

### Post 1: Setting the Stage - The Semester's Premise

- **Title Suggestion:** _The Blank Page and the Thesis: Defining the Challenge_
    
- **Reflection:**
    
    - Start of Semester: What were your initial goals for the studio? (e.g., "I want to master procedural modeling," "I want to explore the poetics of digital decay.")
        
    - Initial Fears: What did you think would be the hardest part?
        
- **Academic Research (Introduction to Core Theme):**
    
    - **Focus:** Introduce your first 3 PDFs. You are using them to _frame_ the semester.
        
    - **Draft Content:** "My initial concept for this project was immediately challenged and defined by a set of foundational readings. Reading $Author A$'s critique of $Concept X$ in [PDF Title] made me realize I needed to focus not just on the _what_ but the _why_ of $Concept X$. Similarly, $Author B$ and $Author C$ provided a critical vocabulary for discussing $Concept Y$ which I now see as the core of my research."
        
- **Creative Practice Research (Initial Inspiration/Precedent):**
    
    - Introduce 3 artists/designers who sparked your initial idea. Include images of their work if possible.
        
    - **Draft Content:** "The visual tone of the project was inspired by $Artist/Designer 1$ (for their use of $Formal Element$), $Artist/Designer 2$ (for their approach to $Media$), and $Artist/Designer 3$ (for their exploration of $Theme$)."
        
- **Technical Research (Tool/Skill Introduction):**
    
    - Name the main tools or software you _knew_ you would need to learn (e.g., a new 3D software, a scripting language).
        
    - **Draft Content:** "The entire project hinges on mastering $Software/Skill$. I spent the first week following foundational tutorials simply learning the interface and the basic operations of $Software/Skill$ before attempting anything complex."
        
- **Project Progress (Project Brief Interpretation):**
    
    - **Draft Content:** "The brief was to [X]. My initial interpretation was to explore [Y]. My initial working title/concept is [Z]."
        

### Post 2: Foundation Building - Challenges and Terminology

- **Title Suggestion:** _From Idea to Action: The First Major Technical Hurdle_
    
- **Reflection:**
    
    - Focus on the **first major technical or conceptual hurdle**. Was it learning a specific command, defining a specific term, or a component of the brief?
        
- **Academic Research (Conceptual Expansion):**
    
    - **Focus:** How your next $\sim$2 PDFs helped you define the specific **terminology** of your work.
        
    - **Draft Content:** "I realized my understanding of $Specific Term$ was too shallow. $Author D$'s definition in [PDF Title] was crucial, highlighting the distinction between $Term A$ and $Term B$, which directly informed my decision to use $Material C$ over $Material D$."
        
- **Creative Practice Research (Technique Analysis):**
    
    - **Draft Content:** "I moved past general appreciation of my precedent artists to _technical analysis_. I researched $Artist/Designer 1$ again, looking specifically at how they achieved the 'glitch effect' in their piece $Piece Name$. This research led me to the technique of $Specific Technique$, which is now a core part of my own project."
        
- **Technical Research (Practice: Basic Implementation):**
    
    - Detail a specific tutorial you followed and **show the resulting practice piece** (even if it's bad!).
        
    - **Draft Content:** "I followed the 'How to Model a $Basic Object$' tutorial on $Platform$. The result was $Result$ , which taught me the importance of $Lesson Learned$ (e.g., clean topology, good naming conventions)."
        
- **Project Progress (Ideation & Prototyping):**
    
    - Show initial sketches, mood boards, or the simplest prototype.
        
    - **Draft Content:** "My early process was visual. Here is the first mood board [Image] which shows the intended color palette, and here are two simple wireframe sketches [Image] of the physical setup. I’m leaning towards $Concept A$."
        

### Post 3: Synthesis & Critique - The First Pivot

- **Title Suggestion:** _The Debate and The Pin-Up: Taking Feedback_
    
- **Reflection:**
    
    - **Mid-Phase Pivot:** What was the biggest piece of feedback you received during an early critique, and how did it force you to change direction?
        
- **Academic Research (Contrasting Views):**
    
    - **Focus:** Discuss a point of debate or conflict between authors in your next $\sim$2 PDFs.
        
    - **Draft Content:** "While $Author E$ argues that $Concept F$ is always $Positive/Negative$, $Author G$ presents a counter-argument that allows for $Nuance$. This theoretical debate mirrors the practical dilemma I'm facing in my own project: $Dilemma$."
        
- **Creative Practice Research (Studio Visit/Talk or Contemporary Analysis):**
    
    - **Draft Content:** "I used this phase to analyze the contemporary work of $Artist/Designer H$'s piece, $Piece Name$. I specifically focused on how they handled the transition between $Element A$ and $Element B$ in their work—a transition I’m currently struggling with in my project."
        
- **Technical Research (Troubleshooting):**
    
    - **Draft Content:** "The biggest technical win this week was finally solving the $Problem$. The problem was $Simple Explanation$. The solution, after hours of searching forums, was to $Specific Solution$ (e.g., change the normals, adjust the UV space, delete the preference file)."
        
- **Project Progress (First Deliverable/Review):**
    
    - Document the outcome of your first critique/deliverable.
        
    - **Draft Content:** "The first formal critique (Pin-Up 1) was valuable. The main feedback was that my concept was too $Too Broad/Too Narrow/Too Technical$. I have since refocused the project to address the emotional core of $Theme$, which will involve $Next Step$."
