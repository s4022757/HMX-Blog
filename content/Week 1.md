# Research

## Academic

The class name AudioVision sounded familiar, so I went back and rediscovered notes I had taken previously about Michel Chion's text.

In Audio-Vision: Sound on screen Chion discusses different ways emotion can be carried in sound in film. He defined many important conceptions in sound design.


> [!summary] Synchresis
> **Synchresis** is the irresistible mental "weld" that occurs when simultaneous auditory and visual phenomena are perceived as causally linked, creating the conviction that the sound belongs to the image. This phenomenon is highly flexible, allowing a wide range of sounds to fit an action and can operate even when sounds and images are technically unrelated, forming powerful, context-dependent audiovisual fusions.


>[!info] **Empathetic Effect:** 
>The music or sound **directly participates** in the scene's emotion, mirroring its **rhythm, tone, and feeling** through recognizable cultural codes (e.g., sad music for a sad scene).


>[!info] **Anempathetic Effect:** 
> The music or sound displays a **conspicuous indifference** to the scene, progressing steadily and undaunted. This juxtaposition **intensifies** the scene's emotion by setting it against an indifferent, often mechanical or "cosmic" background, an effect deemed particularly relevant to the mechanical nature of cinema.
## Creative Practice

Of the works that Darrin showed the AudioVision cohort this week was a sound design he made for a video performance of Japanese Dancer Nobuyoshi Asai. 
![[Darrin Verhagen - Nobuyoshi-0001.png]]
By far this was the work the stuck with me the most. I thought the audio paired with the visual elements in a natural and affective way, there was both enough detail in the layering of each sonic element but also left plenty of space to both focus on and contemplate the emotional narrative of the dance. Having discussed audiovisual contracts and the emotional manipulation that sound offers, I thought that in this piece, the soundtrack amplified the unsettling and uncanny feeling of the piece by being exisiting in a shifting synch to the dance, and at times anempathetic.
