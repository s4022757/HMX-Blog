# Reflection

Time to reframe away from AudioVision and start my journey into Heightened Multisensory Experiences.

We started the week with a trip to the Capitol Theatre and had a demonstration of the Pharos lighting system with some past projects. The space is incredible and I am immediately keen on getting the opportunity to work in this space. The immersive nature of the theatre's roof and the intensity and versatility of the lighting system is really awe inspiring, especially when paired with an immersive sound design. We saw a range of different emotions that projects were able to evoke, using different techniques to do with colour, movement, audiovisual relationships

We discussed much at the theatre such as how affective emotional communication and artistic impact hinge upon a delicate, intentional balance between change and constancy. To prevent wandering attention and ensure audience engagement, one must first maintain a firm anchor point from which you can explore. From this grounding, impact is built not through randomness, but through techniques: building and breaking expectation to pull attention to an element, and carefully building and exploring audio/visual relationships, making sure they are not broken without clear purpose. Playing with constraint and restraint, recognizing that limitations create opportunity spaces for tension to build and for the overall impact to resonate powerfully. This requires considering the whole environment, for instance, drawing attention away from the front and using the entire space. 

I thought one of the most memorable of the project we saw was a more abstract project which successfully anthropomorphised the space. My mind started to fill with ideas about how this could be pushed further, to really make the space breathe perhaps using lighting to replicate some organic element of the theatre. 

The fractal cladding of the roof is striking and I noticed that it was really enhanced with deep saturated reds and blues, which when flashing emphasised the shapes the fractals through the shadows drawn, pairing very well with the more darker and intense projects. The Pharos system and Capitol roof is such a large and impressive spectacle, emphasising the vastness of the space even further and making the audience feel even smaller is definitely a direction I would like to take.

Of the affective states that we must select for our projects, there are multiple that appeal to me and could be created through the concepts I identified during the visit to the Capitol. Here is my short list to look more into:
- **sublime**
- **hypnotizing**
- **terrifying**
- **uncanny**
- **visceral**
- **hypnagogic**
- **surreal**
Sublime sticks out the most to me. A quick google search defines it as:

# Research

## Academic

### Burke - A Philosophical Enquiry into the Origin of Our Ideas of the Sublime and Beautiful 1757


Burke explains that the emotion of the sublime is complex and contrasting. It is characterized as a sort of **delightful horror**, or a **tranquillity tinged with terror**.

He argues that the main source of the sublime is terror, which may be either present or implied, when combined with feelings of astonishment, **admiration, reverence and respect**. When we contemplate a unfathomably object like an omnipotent Deity, "we shrink into the minuteness of our own nature, and are, in a manner, **annihilated before him**," feeling a shade of secret dread and horror.

From this text I understand the experience of the sublime is like standing at the edge of a cliff: you are not yet falling, but the sensation and the irresistible tension, the breathtaking horror, and the suspension of your normal thoughts is produced by the _idea_ of immense danger or overwhelming power, allowing the mind to be aroused without actual harm.

### Doran - The Theory of the Sublime from Longinus to Kant

According to Doran, the term "sublime" itself means "**loftiness, height, or elevation**". It is derived from the Latin _sublimis_, meaning 'elevated,' 'uplifted,' or 'aloft'.

The sublime is characterized by a paradoxical structure of experience: **being simultaneously overwhelmed and elated**.

This experience is typically associated with notions of:
• **Ecstasy**
• **Grandeur**.
• **Terror**.
• **Awe**.
• **Astonishment**
• **Wonder**
• **Admiration**.

The sublime experience is powerful because it "**anticipates our reasonings, and hurries us on by an irresistible force**", exerting an "**invincible power and force**" over the audience

Doran discussed how Kant contributed to the discourse on the sublime. Kant's theory in the _Critique of the Power of Judgment_ (1790) was that the sublime is not found in the object of nature, but only in the **mind of the person judging**. The experience is caused by a **conflict** between the imagination and reason. The imagination is overwhelmed by the magnitude (Mathematically Sublime) or power (Dynamically Sublime) of nature, but our ability to reason simultaneously makes us feel powerful and free because it isn't limited by the physical world.

## Creative Practice

I have to identify a number of works which deal with the sublime for assignment 1 due at the end of the week, so let's explore a few that already come to mind.

### Within Without - James Turrell 
*Installation - Architecture*

Having grown up in Canberra, I have memories from childhood of visiting the National Gallery of Australia, where my favourite of the artworks there is an installation by James Turrell. This architectural installation plays with light and form in a way that invites contemplation and always made me feel small under the overwhelming scale of the sky. It felt like a meditative sanctuary where the atmosphere, natural light, worldly architecture converge. Its sublime emerges in the collapse of immensity into intimacy, framing the sky as both boundless and near. It induces the feeling of slowing time, providing stillness, and amplifying the presence of the audience and their scale by manipulating light and cathedral-like geometry. The work evokes a subtle awe and an encounter with the vast and unknowable.

### X-Verse - Ryoji Ikeda
*Installation - Audio Visual*

The most memorable audiovisual experience of mine which comes to mind is when I went to see Ryoji Ikeda's installation while living in London. Ikeda presents pure data as a visual and sonic experience. The audience is overwhelmed by fast-moving numeric streams, flickering lights, and noise which all evoke the sublime through abstraction, scale, and the digital unknown. The scale of the data that our modern systems have amassed is terrifying, especially when brightly emitted in every direction you look. Beautiful, awe-inspiring, overwhelming.
