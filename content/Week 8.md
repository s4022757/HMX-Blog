# Research

## Academic

By looking through the canvas resources, I cam across the Open Affective Standarized image set (OASIS). This is a free, open-source collection of images available for creative use. Crucially, every image has been rigorously **categorized and rated** according to **Russell's Circumplex Model of Affect**. These ratings map each photo across two core dimensions: **Valence** (positive to negative feeling) and **Arousal** (high to low excitement).

Since I have already mapped the concept of the Sublime onto Russell's Circumplex Model (high-arousal, mixed-valence), this dataset would allow for direct, evidence-based selection of visuals assets to work with. This would allow me to fine-tune visual stimulus to elicit the specific emotional blend in mind (high-arousal, slightly negative terror mixed with the high-arousal, positive awe characteristic of the Sublime).

## Creative Practice

The visit to the Malthouse theatre was a great insight into the professional creative practice of a composer working in the performing arts. The discussion we had helped me to understand the composer's process within a professional, collaborative environment. Seeing how the composer moved from initial script or concept to final score reveals the necessity of adaptability, time management, and communication between the different technical and creative fields involve in producing an affective, cohesive experience.
